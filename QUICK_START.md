# 🚀 Quick Start: Progress Chart Feature

## Where to Find It

Open your QC Calculator (`index.html`) in your browser and scroll to the **very bottom** of the page.

You'll see this new section:
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📈 Progress Chart & Analysis
Track QC performance over time and identify areas requiring improvement
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## 30-Second Quick Start

### Step 1: Save Some Evaluations
- Fill out the QC evaluation form at the top
- Rate the criteria
- Click "Save & Export"
- **Repeat 2-3 times** with different data

### Step 2: Generate Charts
- Scroll to "Progress Chart & Analysis" section
- Click **"Generate Progress Chart"** button
- Wait for charts to load (usually instant)

### Step 3: View Your Progress
- Look at the 4 statistics at the top
- Review the 5 charts that appear
- Read the "Key Insights & Recommendations"
- Check the "Areas Needing Improvement" list

### Step 4 (Optional): Filter & Export
- Apply filters (date, QC type, faculty) if desired
- Export to Excel or PDF to share

## Tips & Tricks

💡 **Tip 1**: Save at least 3-5 evaluations before generating charts for meaningful trends

💡 **Tip 2**: Check trends weekly - small upward trends compound into big improvements

💡 **Tip 3**: Focus on top 3 improvement areas first - don't try to fix everything at once

💡 **Tip 4**: Use faculty filter to benchmark high performers

💡 **Tip 5**: Export reports at key milestones for documentation

💡 **Tip 6**: Share improvement insights with faculty for coaching discussions

## Troubleshooting

### "No evaluations found" message
**Solution**: Save at least one QC evaluation first, then generate charts

### Charts don't update after saving new evaluation
**Solution**: Click the "Generate Progress Chart" button again to refresh

### Can't see the Progress Chart section
**Solution**: Scroll to the very bottom of the page - it's below the history section

### Export not working
**Solution**: Refresh the page and try again. Make sure you have data to export

### Mobile charts look small
**Solution**: They're responsive! The charts automatically adjust to your screen size

---

## What Happens With Your Data

✅ **Saved Locally**: All data stored in your evaluations.json file
✅ **Not Cloud Stored**: Unless you explicitly export, data stays on your device  
✅ **Client-Side Processing**: Charts generated in your browser, not on a server
✅ **Your Control**: You decide when to export and share
